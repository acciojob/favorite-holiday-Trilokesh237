BALI

Bali is an Indonesian island known for its rich culture, stunning landscapes, and vibrant tourism scene. It’s often considered a tropical paradise, with beautiful beaches, lush rice terraces, and majestic volcanoes. Bali is part of the Indonesian archipelago and lies just east of Java.

  1. Uluwatu Temple Perched on a dramatic cliff, Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali’s most iconic sea temples.
  2. Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary (Ubud) Located in Ubud, this lush, tropical forest is home to hundreds of playful long-tailed macaques.
  3. Tegallalang Rice Terraces These beautiful, terraced rice fields in Ubud are a famous spot for visitors to witness Bali's agricultural beauty
  4. Tanah Lot Temple Set on a rock formation in the sea, Tanah Lot is one ofBali’s most photographed temples.
  5. Mount Batur For the adventurous, hiking to the summit ofMount Batur for a sunrise trek is a bucket-list activity.
  • Babi Guling - This is Bali’s version of roasted pork, typically prepared for special occasions
  • Bebek Betutu - A Balinese delicacy where duck is marinated with spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and then slowly cooked.
  • Nasi Campur - Literally meaning "mixed rice," this dish includes a variety of side dishes served with white rice, such asmeats, vegetables, peanuts, eggs, and fried shallots .
  • Sate Lilit - A Balinese take on satay, these skewers are made with minced meat (often fish or chicken) mixed with grated coconut, lime leaves, and spices, then wrapped around a stick and grilled.
